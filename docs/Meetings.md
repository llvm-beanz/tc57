TC57 holds regular teleconferences to discuss and make progress on active
proposals and defects.

All meetings of TC57 are covered by the [Code of Conduct](CodeOfConduct.md).

## Agenda and Format

An agenda will be created for each meeting in the [Meetings] directory. Topics may be added to the agenda by proposing edits to a given agenda's pull request replacing `<placeholder topics>` which appear in the [template](./Meetings/Template.md).

After each meeting, the secretary will prepare minutes in Microsoft Word and Markdown format. Microsoft Word documents will be distributed by the Ecma secretariat to all delegates registered to the committee. The Markdown document will be added to the  [Meetings] directory via a pull request.

The first agenda item for each meeting is to approve the meeting minutes from the last meeting and merge them into the repository.

At the conclusion of each meeting, a pull request for the agenda of the subsequent meeting will be opened.

## Consensus Decision-Making

TC57 uses consensus decision making for all decisions. A single participant
breaking consensus can prevent a proposal or change from advancing. A
participant should not break consent without strong reasoning and willingness to
put effort into the process to resolve the issues.

It is acceptable and appropriate for participants to clearly state their dislike
of a proposal or solution without breaking consent (e.g. "I don't like this
proposal, for _reasons_, but will not break consensus."). Providing this kind of
feedback can highlight significant issues and allow proposal authors the
opportunity to address concerns. Candid and comprehensive constructive feedback
is essential to reaching the high quality bar we strive for.

## Video recording, meeting minutes and legal disclaimer

During the TC meeting, a video recording (using the built-in function of the video conferencing tool, currently Zoom) will be taken to aide in producing and publishing [minutes][Meetings] afterward. The video file is deleted immediately upon the opening of the minutes pull request by the secretary, and is not at any time made public. TC57 participants are encouraged to review and modify minutes for accuracy before the following meeting, at which time the PR is merged. (A PR can be made at any time; it is best to do so while the meeting is still fresh in the mind of the participants.)

Meeting participation is restricted to Delegates of Ecma Members and Invited Experts approved by the Ecma Secretariat. Meeting minutes are available to anyone in the public by navigating to the [Meetings] directory of the [hlsl-tc57/tc57](https://github.com/hlsl-tc57/tc57/) repository on GitHub.
